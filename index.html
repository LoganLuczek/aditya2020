Aditya for College Council President

Currently, I am a third-year studying Neuroscience and Behavioral Biology paired with Quantitative Sciences on an Economics Track.

My involvement with College Council started my first year on campus and has not stopped since then. In my time with CC I have been a legislator, served on the finance and budget committee, and am currently acting as the VP of Finance for the 64th legislature. The projects I have had the opportunity of working on span from helping implement online course evaluations for Emory’s College of Arts and Sciences, organizing Professors at Kaldi’s Getting Coffee—an event that creates student to faculty dialogue in interdisciplinary settings, and working through significant changes in allocating money to organizations.

As I run for President of College Council, I seek to improve Emory University and advocate for communities and organizations on campus.

Opportunity

I am open to questions, concerns, and thoughts on my vision for fostering increased collaboration and entrepreneurship through College Council at Emory University.

Careers

I wish to incentivize students to attend events on Handshake, push for resources in the Career Center, and make career fairs comfortable and approachable.

Innovation

I will increase awareness about competitions and resources that are not easy to learn about outside the Goizueta Business School.

Leadership

I will help facilitate annual leadership summits, the first happening in April, so that campus leaders can continuously collaborate throughout the year. I will also improve our integral relationship with RHA.

Diversity & Inclusion

I am open to questions, concerns, and thoughts on my vision for College Council’s role in diversity and inclusion at Emory University.

Cultural Competency

I will continue active programming to engage all identities at Emory, continue accommodating students, and push for more excused absences on religious holidays.

Awareness

I will reduce potential College Council bias, make members more aware of sexual assault and LGBT resources through SAPA and Safe Space Training.

Oxford

I will visit Oxford SGA GBMs and host the Oxford SGA president for CC GBMs, encourage cross-campus club collaboration, and push for research programs and transition labs.

Finance

I am open to questions, concerns, and thoughts on my vision for College Council’s finance and budgeting processes to create a better system.

Transparency

I will ensure that more clubs are chartered, existing clubs will grow, cut CC executive board costs, and increase communication about monetary policy changes.

Accessibility

I will work with Student Government Services to create easy and simple online reimbursements and use Eaglesource for a centralized online area for all organizations to download financial files.

Training

I will improve treasurer training in order to help in programming events, structuring organizations, and finding resources.
